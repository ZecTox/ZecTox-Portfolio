Selling Globally in 2025: The Ultimate Guide to Shopify Markets Pro

By Tejas Kedare • Updated: Dec 21, 2025 • 18 min read

International sales were once a nightmare of customs forms, surprise import duties, and angry customers refusing packages. Shopify Markets Pro has completely solved this.

In 2025, you can't afford to be just a local business. This guide explains how to turn on 150+ countries overnight without hiring a logistics team.

Chapter 1: Merchant of Record (MoR) Explained

The biggest hurdle to global selling is legal liability. Who is responsible for collecting VAT in France? Who handles GST in Australia? With standard Shopify Markets, you are.

With Markets Pro, Shopify (and their partner Global-e) becomes the "Merchant of Record." They take on the legal liability. You just sell. They handle the compliance, remittance, and tax filing. This feature alone is worth the cost for peace of mind.

Chapter 2: Automating Duties & Taxes

Nothing kills conversion like a customer seeing a "surprise" $50 fee upon delivery. Markets Pro allows you to collect duties at checkout (DDP - Delivered Duty Paid).

This means your Italian customer pays for the shirt + shipping + Italian tax + duties all in one go. The package arrives at their door with no extra fees. This dramatic improvement in customer experience can increase international conversion rates by up to 60%.

Chapter 3: Localized Currency and Content

If you show a price in USD to a shopper in Tokyo, they will likely bounce. Markets Pro automatically:

  • Converts prices to the local currency (JPY, EUR, GBP, etc.) with rounding rules (e.g. €50.00 instead of €49.12).
  • Translates checkout fields to the local language.
  • Shows local payment methods (e.g., iDEAL in Netherlands, Bancontact in Belgium).

Chapter 4: International Shipping Rates

Because Markets Pro aggregates volume from thousands of merchants, you get access to DHL Express rates that are often cheaper than standard post.

  • Speed: 2-5 day delivery to almost anywhere in the world.
  • Tracking: Full end-to-end visibility.
  • Paperless Trade: Customs invoices are generated and submitted digitally. No more sticking 5 copies of paper to the outside of the box.

Conclusion: Go Global Today

The world is waiting for your products. With Shopify Markets Pro, the technical and legal barriers have been removed. The only limit is your marketing reach.

Markets Pro vs Standard Shopify Markets

It's important to understand that Shopify offers two tiers of international selling:

  • Shopify Markets (Standard): Available on all plans. Handles multi-currency, basic translation, and international pricing. You handle duties/taxes yourself.
  • Shopify Markets Pro: A managed solution where Shopify acts as the Merchant of Record for international orders. They handle duties, taxes, compliance, and currency conversion automatically.

When to Use Markets Pro

Markets Pro is ideal when you're selling to countries with complex import regulations (EU, UK, Canada, Australia). The key benefit is that customers see guaranteed landed costs at checkout—no surprise customs fees at delivery. This dramatically reduces international cart abandonment.

Setting Up Multi-Currency Pricing

One of the most common mistakes I see is merchants using automatic currency conversion without adjusting for market expectations. For example, a $49.99 USD product might convert to €47.23 in Europe. That's an ugly number. Instead, set manual "rounded" prices: €49.99. This involves using Shopify's price lists feature to set per-market pricing.

Translation and Localization Best Practices

Translation is more than converting words. It's about cultural adaptation:

  • Product sizes: Convert US sizes to EU/UK equivalents. Don't make customers do mental math.
  • Date formats: US uses MM/DD/YYYY, most of the world uses DD/MM/YYYY.
  • Measurement units: Use cm/kg for non-US markets, not inches/pounds.
  • Payment methods: Enable iDEAL for Netherlands, Bancontact for Belgium, Klarna for Scandinavia.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Shopify Markets Pro cost?

Markets Pro charges a 6.5% transaction fee on international orders plus a 2.5% currency conversion fee. This sounds high, but compare it to the cost of setting up separate international stores, compliance, and duty calculation services—it's usually cheaper for low-to-medium volume international sales.

Can I use Markets Pro on Shopify Standard plans?

Yes, Markets Pro is available across all Shopify plans, not just Plus. However, the transaction fees apply on top of your regular Shopify plan fees.

How does Markets Pro handle returns?

Returns are processed through Shopify's standard returns system. However, since Shopify acts as the Merchant of Record, duties paid by the customer may need separate refund handling. I recommend setting up a clear international returns policy before enabling Markets Pro.

Will international SEO be affected?

Shopify Markets uses subfolder structure (e.g., /en-gb/, /fr/) with proper hreflang tags. This is the Google-recommended approach for multi-region SEO. For more details, see my Technical SEO Checklist.
