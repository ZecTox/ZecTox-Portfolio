BigCommerce to Shopify Migration: The Complete 2026 Guide

By Tejas Kedare • 12 min read • March 18, 2026

Thinking about switching from BigCommerce to Shopify? You're not alone. In 2026, more brands than ever are making the jump — and for good reason. Shopify's ecosystem, app marketplace, and developer community have matured into the most powerful e-commerce platform available today.

But migration isn't just "export and import." It's a complex process involving product data, customer records, order history, SEO preservation, and custom functionality. I recently completed a full BigCommerce-to-Shopify migration for SpiceZen, an Australian spice brand — migrating 300+ products, 10,000+ customers, and complex pincode-based shipping rates. This guide shares everything I learned.

Why Brands Are Leaving BigCommerce in 2026

BigCommerce is a solid platform, but several trends are pushing merchants toward Shopify:

  • App Ecosystem Gap: Shopify's App Store has 10,000+ apps compared to BigCommerce's ~1,200. Critical tools for subscriptions, loyalty, and upselling are Shopify-first.
  • Developer Talent Pool: Finding a BigCommerce developer is significantly harder and more expensive than finding Shopify talent. The community is smaller.
  • Checkout Customization: Shopify's Checkout Extensibility (and checkout.liquid for Plus) offers far deeper customization than BigCommerce's Optimized One-Page Checkout.
  • Theme Flexibility: Shopify's Online Store 2.0 architecture with sections and blocks gives merchants drag-and-drop control that BigCommerce's Stencil themes can't match.
  • AI & Automation: Shopify Magic, Sidekick, and native AI tools are years ahead of anything BigCommerce offers.

The Migration Roadmap: Phase by Phase

A successful migration isn't a weekend project. Here's the proven process I follow:

Phase 1: Audit & Planning (Week 1)

Before touching any data, you need a complete picture of what exists on BigCommerce:

  • Product Catalog Audit: Count products, variants, categories, custom fields, and metafields. For SpiceZen, this was 300+ products across 20+ categories with weight-based pricing.
  • Customer Data Mapping: Identify customer groups, addresses, order history, and loyalty points. Note: customer passwords cannot be migrated — plan a re-registration flow.
  • URL Structure Documentation: Map every BigCommerce URL to its future Shopify equivalent. This is critical for SEO.
  • Third-Party Integrations: List every app, webhook, and API integration. Find Shopify equivalents.
  • Custom Functionality: Document any custom code, Stencil modifications, or script manager scripts.

Phase 2: Shopify Store Setup (Week 2)

Build the foundation on Shopify before migrating any data:

  • Theme Selection & Customization: Choose a theme that matches your brand. For complex stores, I build custom sections rather than relying on page builders. This keeps the store fast.
  • Navigation Architecture: Recreate your menu structure, mega menus, and footer links.
  • Payment Gateway Setup: Configure Shopify Payments, PayPal, or region-specific gateways. For Australian stores, Afterpay and PayPal are essential.
  • Shipping Configuration: This is often the most complex part. More on this below.

Phase 3: Data Migration (Week 3-4)

The core migration process — this is where precision matters:

Product Migration

BigCommerce exports products as CSV, but the format doesn't map 1:1 to Shopify's import format. Key differences:

  • BigCommerce uses "categories" as a text field; Shopify uses "collections" as separate entities
  • Product images need to be re-hosted or URL-mapped
  • Custom fields become Shopify metafields (requires mapping)
  • Variant structures may differ — BigCommerce allows more options per product

Pro Tip: I use a combination of Shopify's native CSV import and the Matrixify app for bulk operations. Matrixify handles complex variant structures and metafield mapping far better than Shopify's default importer.

Customer Data Migration

For SpiceZen, we migrated 10,000+ customer records including:

  • Names, emails, and phone numbers
  • Multiple shipping addresses per customer
  • Order history (for reference, not re-processing)
  • Customer tags for segmentation

Important: Shopify requires customers to reset their passwords after migration. Send a branded "Welcome to our new store" email with a password reset link. I use Shopify Flow to automate this on the migration day.

Order History

Historical orders can be imported to Shopify for reference using the Matrixify app. This ensures your customer service team has full order context without losing any data.

Phase 4: Custom Shipping Rates Setup

This was the most complex part of the SpiceZen migration. The store had pincode-based custom shipping rates across Australia — different rates for metro, regional, and remote areas.

Shopify's native shipping profiles handle most scenarios, but for pincode-level granularity, I implemented:

  • Shipping Zones: Created detailed zones matching Australian postal code ranges
  • Weight-Based Rates: Different rates per zone based on order weight
  • Free Shipping Thresholds: Conditional free shipping for orders over a certain value per zone
  • Third-Party Carrier Integration: Connected Australia Post and StarTrack for real-time calculated rates on specific routes

Phase 5: SEO Preservation (Critical!)

The #1 mistake in any migration is losing organic traffic. Here's how to prevent it:

  • 301 Redirects: Create a redirect for every single old URL. BigCommerce uses /products/product-name/, Shopify uses /products/product-handle. Map them all.
  • Meta Tags: Transfer all title tags and meta descriptions. Don't let Shopify auto-generate them.
  • Canonical Tags: Verify Shopify's canonical tag implementation, especially for collection-filtered product URLs.
  • Structured Data: Re-implement JSON-LD for Product, BreadcrumbList, and Organization schemas.
  • Google Search Console: Submit the new sitemap and monitor indexing for 4-6 weeks post-migration.

Phase 6: Testing & Launch (Week 5)

Never launch without thorough testing:

  • Place test orders across all payment methods
  • Verify shipping rate calculations for multiple addresses
  • Test on mobile devices (iPhone, Android, tablet)
  • Check all redirects are working
  • Verify Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, and other tracking
  • Test customer login and password reset flow
  • Load test the store with expected traffic volumes

Common Migration Pitfalls (And How to Avoid Them)

  • Rushing the timeline: A proper migration takes 4-6 weeks. Cutting corners leads to data loss and broken SEO.
  • Ignoring mobile: 70%+ of your customers are on mobile. Test the new store on real devices, not just browser emulators.
  • Forgetting email flows: Re-setup your transactional emails, abandoned cart sequences, and marketing automations on Shopify.
  • Not communicating with customers: Send an email announcing the new store. Frame it positively — "We've upgraded our shopping experience!"
  • Skipping the redirect map: Every. Single. URL. Must. Redirect. One broken link can tank your rankings.

Here's what we achieved with the SpiceZen BigCommerce-to-Shopify migration:

  • ✅ 300+ products migrated with zero data loss
  • ✅ 10,000+ customer records transferred seamlessly
  • ✅ Custom pincode-based shipping replicated and improved
  • ✅ Custom-designed website built from scratch per client specs
  • ✅ Zero downtime during the cutover
  • ✅ SEO preserved — no traffic drop post-migration

Should You Migrate? A Decision Framework

Consider migrating from BigCommerce to Shopify if:

  • You're spending too much time looking for BigCommerce apps that don't exist
  • Your developer bills are high because BigCommerce talent is scarce
  • You want AI-powered tools for product descriptions, customer service, and analytics
  • You need checkout customization beyond what BigCommerce offers
  • You're scaling internationally and want Shopify Markets

Conclusion: Migration Is an Investment, Not an Expense

A platform migration is a big decision. Done right, it's a growth catalyst — a faster store, better apps, more flexibility, and a platform that's investing billions in your success. Done wrong, it's a nightmare of broken links and lost customers.
